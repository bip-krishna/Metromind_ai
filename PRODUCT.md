# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Metro operations controllers, hackathon judges evaluating operational clarity, and KMRCL decision makers. Users are working under time pressure in a control-room context where they need to identify risk, inspect stations, simulate disruptions, and understand recommended actions quickly.

## Product Purpose

MetroMind AI is a Kochi Metro operations digital twin. It exists to make network state observable, forecast pressure deterministically, recommend operational actions, and explain those actions through an AI copilot. Success means a user can understand the corridor status within seconds, drill into a station, run a scenario, and see predictions, alerts, recommendations, and explanations change together.

## Brand Personality

Commanding, precise, calm. The product should feel like real operations software: mission-control confidence, map-led spatial intelligence, restrained urgency, and high trust. It should impress hackathon judges by making the whole digital twin pipeline visible without becoming theatrical.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic Grafana or BI-dashboard layouts, disconnected KPI card grids, rainbow gradients, decorative glass effects, generic chat widgets hidden in corners, raw tables as the primary experience, and motion that exists only for spectacle. The interface should not look like a commuter app or a marketing landing page.

## Design Principles

- Map first: the live metro map is the primary control surface, not a supporting chart.
- One glance status: expose network health, critical alerts, prediction horizon, and highest-risk station immediately.
- Simulate as story: scenario playback should show the Observe -> Predict -> Recommend -> Explain chain in one visible flow.
- Explain the action: recommendations and alerts must always connect station, cause, impact, and confidence.
- Operational restraint: use color, animation, and density to communicate state changes, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Status must not rely on color alone; pair color with labels, icons, and severity text. Support reduced motion by keeping animated trains and scenario transitions non-essential. Keep controls keyboard accessible and preserve readable font sizes on tablet and desktop.
